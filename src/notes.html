Data notes for Washington state graphics

LATEST:

Data is as of 11:59 p.m. Nov. 24 and is preliminary, as duplicate new cases may be included due to unprecedentedly high testing volumes.

CONFIRMED CASES:

"Total confirmed cases" data includes deaths. Number of recoveries is not recorded by the DOH.


DEATHS:

As of Aug. 29, the DOH stopped reporting deaths on the weekends. Those deaths are added to Monday's and Tuesday's counts.

The daily total shows the net change per day in the stateâ€™s death count. DOH reports new deaths while removing deaths from the total when the primary cause of death is not the coronavirus.


Daily updates were not provided for:

  • July 10, 30
  • Sept. 7
  • Oct. 10, 11
  • Nov. 10, 21

These days contained previous day's case data and are not included in the 'All counties' view of the new cases graph:

  • March 28
  • Nov. 22

Data backlogs increased death counts on these days:

  • July 27
  • Sept. 8, 28
  • Nov. 22

Other notable anomalies:

  • Data for Aug. 16 is as of 4:30 p.m., not 11:59 p.m., because routine maintenance interrupted lab-report processing at the DOH on that day. This likely caused a lower count of cases and deaths for Aug. 16.
  • DOH data cleaning on April 18 removed 190 confirmed cases that were discovered to be out-of-state residents.
